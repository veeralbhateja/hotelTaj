Find Yourself Inside of The Royal Hunting Lodge

The famous Ranthambore National Park is the best place to spot the elusive wild tigers of Rajasthan. The 1334 sq km park, with the picturesque ruins of the 10th-century Ranthambore Fort at its centre, was a Maharaja’s hunting ground until 1970. The wild jungle scrublands of the park are hemmed in by rocky ridges, making it the ideal natural expanse for its various inhabitants.


Situated 12.1 kilometres from the Ranthambore National Park, the Vivanta Sawai Madhopur Lodge, is the only heritage hotel in Ranthambore. Its status as a former hunting lodge is clearly visible, at this luxurious property.
